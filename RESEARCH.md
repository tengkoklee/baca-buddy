## Part 1 — Verified science (3-0 adversarial verification)

### 1.1 Dyslexia is script-specific — one generic approach won't work  *(HIGH confidence)*

| Deficit | Scope | Hits which language |
|---|---|---|
| Phonological awareness (PA) | **universal** | all three |
| Rapid automatized naming (RAN) | **universal** | all three |
| Phonemic / decoding | script-specific | **English** (opaque orthography) — bites hardest |
| Visual-orthographic + morphological | script-specific | **Chinese** hanzi |
| Fluency/speed (accuracy resolves fast) | transparency effect | **Malay** — accuracy ceiling comes quickly, *slowness* persists |

Sources: Annual Review of Linguistics 2023 (Zhang et al.); Ho & Fong 2005; Kuester-Gruber et al. PLoS ONE 2023; Lee et al. Frontiers 2020 (Malaysia, n=866 Grade-1).

### 1.2 Chinese dyslexia needs morphology + character-structure work, not just sound work  *(HIGH)*

- Morphological awareness (MA) is a **core, script-specific construct** — driven by Chinese's
  massive homophone load. In 223 dyslexic Beijing children (9–11yo), MA was the strongest
  single predictor (OR = 0.25). Subtypes are heterogeneous (global 21.5%, phonological 30.9%,
  RAN 15.7%, mild morphological 31.8%).
- Phonology still matters in Chinese: 83.6% of dyslexic children showed ≥1 phonological deficit.
- Visual memory correlates with Chinese literacy (DAS Singapore pilot, rs=.33).
- **Refuted claim** (0-3): "rote copying is useless" — do NOT throw out tracing/copying entirely;
  the evidence didn't support ranking it as least effective.

### 1.3 Expect difficulty in all three languages, and use phonics as the shared lever  *(HIGH / MEDIUM)*

- Co-occurrence: L1-Chinese dyslexic children reliably struggle with English too (and vice versa).
  Caveat: ~30–40% of bilingual poor readers are impaired in both — individual dissociation happens.
- **Asymmetric transfer** (single RCT, Yeung et al. 2023, HK): 8-week **English phonics training
  improved BOTH English AND Chinese word reading**; Chinese orthographic training did NOT
  transfer to English. → Systematic alphabetic phonics is the highest-leverage shared foundation.
  (Medium confidence: one study, younger kids, effects may fade.)

### 1.4 Proven intervention components  *(HIGH)*

- **Chinese character-structure training** (radicals, legal radical positions, stroke patterns) +
  morpheme/meaning awareness: "unambiguously positive" (Annual Review 2023); RCT meta-analysis
  g = 0.70, **dyslexics benefit more (g = 0.87)** (Ruan, Maurer & McBride 2024).
- **Orton-Gillingham-adapted multisensory hanzi remediation** (games, storytelling, tracing):
  significant gains in Chinese literacy/character reading/vocabulary (n=16, uncontrolled — treat gently).
- **Malay**: Malaysian classrooms teach letter-*name* → syllable → morpheme with **no explicit
  grapheme-phoneme (phonics) instruction** (2-1 verification split — "typical" classrooms).
  → A home letter-*sound* phonics module **supplements rather than duplicates** school.
  Phonological-decoding was the strongest at-risk discriminator in the Malaysian sample (0.573).

### 1.5 Digital intervention evidence (from fetch layer; consistent, mostly meta-analyses)

- **Phonics instruction is the ONLY approach with statistically confirmed efficacy** for reading
  disabilities across 22 RCTs (reading g′ = 0.322; spelling g′ = 0.336). After publication-bias
  correction: g′ ≈ 0.198 — real but modest; calibrate expectations. (Galuschka et al.)
- Phonemic-awareness-only, fluency-only, comprehension-only, auditory training, coloured
  overlays: **none reached significance**.
- **TTS/read-aloud**: d = .35 (bias-corrected .24) for comprehension — synthesized voice works
  as well as human; benefit holds at primary age. BUT it's a *compensatory access tool*, not a
  decoding teacher — don't rely on TTS alone.
- **GraphoGame trials**: letter-sound knowledge gains confirmed; fluency transfer weak and
  dose-dependent. Design lessons: secure PA foundations first, **>15 hours total practice**,
  above-average exposure needed for fluency effects, embed assessment into the game.
- Computer-delivered + adult supervision ≈ group intervention effectiveness (g′ = 0.364 vs 0.379).
- Evidence base is 69% English / 15% Spanish — very thin for Malay & Chinese digital tools.

## Part 2 — KSSR Semakan 2017, Tahun 3 SJKC (primary-source claims, NOT adversarially verified)

**Critical routing fact: SJK schools follow their OWN DSKP documents — always use the
SJK/SJKC version, never the SK version.** Three separate documents: 华文 (Bahasa Cina),
国文 (BM for SJK), 英文 (English for SJK). Authoritative source: MOE BPK portal
(bpk.moe.gov.my → kurikulum sekolah rendah).

### Bahasa Cina 华文 (from DSKP KSSR Semakan 2017 BC Tahun 3)
- End-of-Year-3 target: **recognize ≈400 hanzi**; write with hard pen AND brush.
- **Pinyin is a decoding aid only** — recognize + pronounce syllables; systematic pinyin
  mastery explicitly NOT required. → App should treat pinyin as pronunciation scaffolding.
- Five modules: 听说 listening-speaking · 阅读 reading · 书写 writing · 趣味语文 fun language ·
  语文基础知识 foundational knowledge.
- Writing standards: basic strokes, stroke order, common radicals/components; standard 4.5.1 =
  brush strokes + tracing kaishu copybooks.
- Assessment: 6-level mastery scale (Tahap Penguasaan / 表现标准 TP1–TP6).

### English (DSKP KSSR Semakan 2017 BI Tahun 3 SJK; direct PDF found at asiemodel.net)
- Target: **CEFR A1 Mid**.
- Core vocabulary = defined wordlist from **Cambridge English Corpus A1 learner data**,
  organised by category (adjectives, animals, body, classroom objects, colours, clothes,
  family, food …). **100% spelling accuracy explicitly NOT required.**
- Remedial phonics pathway for struggling pupils: revisit **Years 1–2 sound-letter tables in
  fixed order** (Row 'a' Y1 → Row 'o' Y2). Official phonics Schemes of Work exist only for
  Y1–Y2 → a dyslexic Y3 child should **backfill Y1–2 phonics**.
- SJK pupils get only **≥80 hours English/year** (reduced vs SK) — home practice matters.
- Skills: Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing + Grammar + Language Arts, oral-first,
  phonics as the stated reading strategy.

### Bahasa Melayu (DSKP BM Tahun 3 SJK — distinct from SK version)
- SJK-specific DSKP PDF (Google Drive: 1mrUo8OWUkw9um5bxLNZM81oOlrlxhgai via gurubesar.my).
- Standard structure: Standard Kandungan → Standard Pembelajaran → Standard Prestasi (TP1-6).
- Vocabulary/skill detail still to be extracted from the actual PDF.

  Assessment ≈ RM800–1500 (by Dyslexia SEN teachers, reports accepted by schools/exam boards).
  Pathway: symptoms → assessment → enrolment → IEP → 3-month intervention. OG-based programmes.
- **MOE working definition** for school screening: unable to master reading despite ≥6 months
  of adequate formal instruction.
- National literacy screening historically via **LINUS** (its English modules are the DSKP's
  recommended remedial material); LINUS-era instruments still circulate in remedial teaching.

## Part 4 — What this means for Baca Buddy (v2 design brief)

Ranked by evidence strength × syllabus fit:

1. **English systematic phonics module** (letter-sound + blending, KSSR Y1–2 scope-and-sequence
   backfill) — the ONLY statistically confirmed intervention class AND the one shown to transfer
   to Chinese. This becomes the app's spine.
2. **Chinese character-structure module** — build characters from radicals/components
   (福 = 礻+ 一 + 口 + 田), legal radical positions, stroke-order tracing; morpheme/homophone
   games (same-sound-different-meaning). Keep pinyin visible as scaffold only. Target the
   ~400-character Tahun 3 list.
3. **Malay letter-SOUND phonics + fluency work** — supplements the school's letter-name method;
   add timed gentle re-reading for fluency (the persistent Malay deficit).
4. **Word banks re-based on the three DSKPs** (English A1-Mid category wordlist; BC 400-char
   list; BM Tahun 3 SJK senarai kata) instead of generic themes.
5. **Keep**: TTS-everywhere (d=.35 compensatory), multisensory hear-see-do, gentle feedback.
   **Adjust**: de-emphasise perfect spelling in English (not required at A1 Mid); report
   progress on a TP1–TP6-style scale; build for ≥15 h cumulative dose via short daily sessions;
   embed light in-game assessment (GraphoGame pattern).
6. **Recommend to parents**: if not yet formally assessed, PDM assessment (~RM800–1500) gives a
   school-recognized report + IEP; school-side, the MOE 6-month criterion triggers screening.

### Verification notes / caveats
- Population mismatch: most Chinese-dyslexia evidence is from China/HK/Singapore
  (English-medium); SJKC is Chinese-medium — transfer ratios may differ.
- The phonics-transfer asymmetry rests on ONE 8-week RCT of ~6–7yo Cantonese speakers.
- OG-hanzi result is uncontrolled pre-post, n=16, publisher-run.
- 4 claims were killed in verification, incl. "PA training only works under age 10" and
  "rote copying is least effective" — do not repeat these.
